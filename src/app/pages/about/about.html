Abstract

The current process for stage testing across FMW product family involves manual regress execution, aggregating code coverage from multiple regress suites, globalization regress execution for multiple locales, dif analysis followed by sapphire upload. The article proposes a process to centralize and automate the workflow for stage testing. The component teams shall involve only analyzing new failures.

Current Overhead

  • Stage testing manual, repetitive and monotonous.
  • At least two person-day, every week (stage announcement frequency) from each product QA team is dedicated for stage testing and coverage report generation alone.
  • Human errors while copying shiphomes form wiki page or while announcing.

RM uses ASTC to trigger stage announcements

StageRunner can have a UI for RM to publish the stage shiphomes. Once announced, the details shall be published in the site and shall also be available as a JSON object that can be readily consumed using a REST request.

How StageRunner Works?

  • StageRunner shall submit jobs to form in parallel and capture coverage for all products. 
  • StageRunner shall email QA regarding the job submissions for the new stage. QA/RM can visually monitor progress of the jobs submitted.
  • StageRunner shall poll for all jobs to complete, consolidate the coverage dump from each job and keep it in common location and generate a unified coverage report.
  • StageRunner shall verify the regress results with pre-configured acceptable range, pre-configured dif analysis, auto upload the qualifying suite results to sapphire and notify QA by email.
  • QA can work on any failing suites or unexpected diffs by accessing work locations preserved by StageRunner.
  • QA/RM can resubmit failed jobs form StageRunner UI.

What does StageRunner accomplish?

  • StageRunner shall make stage shiphome details available as a REST resource to be consumed by any tool (Including StageRunner itself)
  • StageRunner shall automate repetitive job of stage regress execution, coverage consolidation and result publishing. For example, in case of Oracle Traffic Director (relatively small product), with 6 regress suites, 20 man-hours per week were saved.
  • QA can focus automation rather than manual stage execution.
